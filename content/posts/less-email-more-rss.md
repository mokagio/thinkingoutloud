The mechanics of having an email list are detrimental to my writing and only recently I remembered that a blog does not _need_ an email list.

This post is not worth an email.
Yet, I still want to _publish_ it.

I value my inbox and apply the same respect to other people's inboxes.
When I send an email, I want it to be worth reading.
While that's a worthy goal in and of itself, it raises the quality bar in a way that prevents the kind of shorter, introspective, open ended, thinking-out-loud posts I find myself wanting to write.

What if I'm writing to feel the keys clicking under my fingers and to coalesce thoughts in the editing process?
What if my writing is just me thinking out loud and finding pleasure in seeing those thoughts on a web page instead of an entry in an offline journal?

A newsletter also comes with a cost that I feel I'd need to recoup somehow.
Ads?
Yuck!
Selling something?
But what can you sell when you are writing to figure things out yourself?

Luckily, there's this old tech that went out of vogue but that might be ready for a comeback: _RSS_.
