I enjoyed the YouTube essay [How Anime Made Superheroes Irrelevant](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bMjTUDBOeQk) arguing that Japanese stories in anime are more compelling for audiences than modern Western superheroes.

One crucial point of difference is how anime and superhero movies treat source material.
Anime is a faithful adaptation of the manga that originated it.
Conversely, a superhero movie is a new interpretation of the characters.
The writers of some Disney+ shows infamously said they didn't even read the comics.

The essay also notices how anime is a gateway to manga, and TV and movies are a gateway to comics, echoed by another video essay, [How Manga Beat Comics For The Next Generation](https://youtu.be/l8Ygl8hyqJs?si=sPSZffiAW6bkS-tJ), which reports massive spikes in manga sales when its anime gains popularity.

I prefer going the other way around:
First read the manga, then watch the anime.

As a matter of fact, I rarely watch anime.
You might be thinking I'm a snob, and I probably am, but I find the reading experience has something that no animation or movie can compare to.

When you read, you create a unique world together with the author that exists only in your head and only at that point in time.
Granted, manga and comics do a lot of heavy lifting, but it's still up to the reader to bring the story to life in their head.

A great anime or movie can deliver a rich and engaging experience—I get goosebumps every time I re-watch the epic Rock Lee vs. Gaara fight—but I just feel there is more magic in what reading gives you.

Reading is also active, while watching is, _mostly_, passive.
And boy do we know to [keep our brains active these days](https://giolodi.com/p/a-training-plan-for-cognitive-fitness).

On top of that, over-reliance on CGI and the jerky feeling of modern 2D animation doesn't help.
I'd rather read a black and white manga than sit through 20 minutes of most recent anime shows.

Look, you do you.
I'm not here to judge, even though I sound like I might.
But I think folks are missing out if they only watch the adaptation instead of reading the original.

Go manga first, and you'll enjoy the anime even more.
