---
title: "Why I Am Learning Japanese"
date: 2026-04-27
---

Two weeks ago, I started learning Japanese.

It was a spur of the moment decision.
I'd landed on a YouTube video titled something like "why you need to watch Legend of the Galactic Heroes" and was captivated by the anime.
I checked it out but only found a version with original audio and English subs.
It didn't feel right and I wished I could enjoy it without subs.

So, why not?
TV shows were my gateway into learning English some twenty years ago.
Maybe anime can teach me Japanese now.

The experience of having reached high proficiency with a second language already makes the whole process easier, and so does the wider array of tools for learning available today, chief among them AI.
I know about spaced repetition and comprehensible input.
I know that Duolingo and other learning apps are basically pointless, as they're optimized for getting you to subscribe and stay subscribed, not to teach you.
I know that following a program is good, but creating my own is better because learning is personal.
[Knowledge is networked](https://www.giolodi.com/p/knowledge-is-networked?utm_source=publication-search) and the infinitely patient AI sensei is happy to go off on tangents with me to add new nodes to the network.

Two weeks in, and I'm able—with constant dictionary lookups and AI explanations—to understand the simplest of the [tadoku.org](https://tadoku.org) level 0 graded books and pick up a few words when listening to [Nihongo con Teppei](https://nihongoconteppei.com/).

The progress is small, but tangible nonetheless.
And at a time when few other things seem to progress, it's a rewarding experience.
