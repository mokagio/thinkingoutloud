---
title: "Why I Am Learning Japanese"
date: 2026-04-27
---

Two weeks ago, I started learning Japanese.

It was a spur of the moment decision.
I'd landed on a YouTube video along the lines of "why you need to watch Legend of the Galactic Heroes" and was captivated by the anime.
I checked it out but only found a version with original audio and English subs.
It didn't feel right and I wished I could enjoy them without subs.

So, why not.
After all, TV shows were my gateway into learning English some twenty years ago.
It's time to pick up something new.

In fact, I already tried to learn Japanese in my late teens.
Back then, I didn't have the tools or the motivation to succeed and my attempt lasted less than a week.

This time around, I have the experience of having reached high proficiency with a second language and have a much wider array of tools to support my learning, chief among it AI.
I know about spaced repetition and comprehensible input.
I know that Duolingo and other learning apps are pointless, because they're optimized for getting you to subscribe and stay subscribed, not to teach you.
I know that following a program is good, but creating your own program is better.

Two weeks in, and I'm able—with constant of dictionary lookups and AI explanations—to understand the simplest of the [tadoku.org](https://tadoku.org) level 0 graded books and picking up a few words when listening to [Nihongo con Teppei](https://nihongoconteppei.com/).

The progress is small, but tangible nonetheless.
And at a time where few other things seem to progress, it's a rewarding experience.
